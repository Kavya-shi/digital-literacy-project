Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick users into sharing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source. It usually happens through emails, messages, or fake websites.

A typical phishing attack starts when a user receives an email or SMS that appears to be from a bank or a popular service. The message often contains a link and asks the user to update or verify their account details. When the user clicks the link, they are redirected to a fake website that looks real. The user then enters their personal information, which is captured by the attacker.

Students and working professionals are common targets because they frequently use online services and may not always verify links. The consequences of phishing include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to accounts.

To avoid phishing, users should always check the sender’s email address, avoid clicking on unknown links, and verify websites before entering sensitive data. Awareness is the key to preventing such cybercrimes.
